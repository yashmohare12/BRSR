# BRSR Carbon Reporting - Quick Reference Guide

## üìã SEBI BRSR Requirements Overview

### Who Must Report?
- Top 1,000 listed companies by market capitalization (as of 2023-24)
- Mandatory disclosure of GHG emissions (Scope 1, 2, and 3)
- Annual reporting requirement

### What to Report?
1. **Scope 1**: Direct GHG emissions from sources owned/controlled by the company
2. **Scope 2**: Indirect emissions from purchased electricity
3. **Scope 3**: All other indirect emissions in the value chain
4. **Emission Intensity**: Emissions per unit of revenue (tCO‚ÇÇe/‚ÇπCrore)

## ‚úÖ BRSR Compliance Checklist

- [ ] Gather all fuel purchase records
- [ ] Collect electricity bills for reporting period
- [ ] Compile travel and expense reports
- [ ] Obtain waste management data
- [ ] Review water consumption records
- [ ] Check procurement records for paper/supplies

### Calculation Phase
- [ ] Enter Scope 1 data (all fuel types)
- [ ] Enter Scope 2 data (electricity)
- [ ] Enter Scope 3 data (travel, waste, etc.)
- [ ] Verify all units of measurement
- [ ] Calculate total emissions
- [ ] Calculate emission intensity

### Documentation Phase
- [ ] Generate emissions report
- [ ] Review calculation methodology
- [ ] Document data sources
- [ ] Keep audit trail of all inputs
- [ ] Archive supporting documents

### Reporting Phase
- [ ] Include emissions in BRSR report
- [ ] Compare with previous year (if applicable)
- [ ] Set reduction targets
- [ ] Plan improvement initiatives
- [ ] Submit to SEBI via BSE/NSE

## üéØ Best Practices

### 1. Data Quality
- Use primary data wherever possible (actual readings)
- Document all assumptions
- Keep records for at least 5 years
- Implement monitoring systems

### 2. Accuracy
- Use location-specific emission factors when available
- Update factors annually
- Consider seasonal variations
- Account for all significant sources

### 3. Verification
- Cross-check calculations
- Perform reasonability checks
- Compare year-over-year
- Consider third-party verification

### 4. Continuous Improvement
- Set reduction targets (e.g., 5% year-over-year)
- Identify high-emission areas
- Implement efficiency measures
- Track progress monthly

## ‚ö†Ô∏è Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Incorrect Units**
   - Mixing liters and gallons
   - kWh vs. MWh confusion
   - Tonnes vs. kg

2. **Double Counting**
   - Counting same emission in multiple scopes
   - Including already offset emissions

3. **Incomplete Data**
   - Missing small sources
   - Not accounting for all facilities
   - Partial year data

4. **Wrong Emission Factors**
   - Using international factors for India
   - Outdated factors
   - Incorrect fuel types

5. **Poor Documentation**
   - No audit trail
   - Missing source documents
   - Unclear assumptions

## üí° Pro Tips

1. **Start Simple**: Begin with the largest emission sources (usually electricity and fuel)

2. **Automate**: Set up automatic data collection systems for recurring measurements

3. **Benchmark**: Compare your emissions with industry peers

4. **Engage Teams**: Involve operations, facilities, and finance teams

5. **Regular Updates**: Track emissions monthly, not just annually

6. **Set Targets**: Align with Science-Based Targets Initiative (SBTi)

7. **Communicate**: Share progress with stakeholders

## üìñ Glossary

**CO‚ÇÇe (Carbon Dioxide Equivalent)**: Standard unit to compare emissions from various greenhouse gases based on global warming potential

**Scope 1**: Direct emissions from owned/controlled sources
**Scope 2**: Indirect emissions from purchased energy
**Scope 3**: All other indirect emissions in value chain

**GHG**: Greenhouse Gas (CO‚ÇÇ, CH‚ÇÑ, N‚ÇÇO, etc.)
**BRSR**: Business Responsibility and Sustainability Reporting
**SEBI**: Securities and Exchange Board of India
**CEA**: Central Electricity Authority

**Emission Factor**: Amount of GHG emitted per unit of activity
**Emission Intensity**: Emissions per unit of economic output
